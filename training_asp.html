AUDIO SIGNAL PROCESSING

Sound is a part of more application areas than ever before. In a typical car — there’s the music and entertainment system (CD player, traditional and/or satellite radio, MP3 player, DVD player), the navigation system, Bluetooth connectivity with the smart phone, and typical alert-related sounds that chime when, for instance, the headlights are left on. In fact, a car typically boasts 10 to 15 different audio algorithms controlling each of these different functions. Moreover, sounds are not simply released in a vehicle; voice command and control functions, such as hands-free calling, mean that always-on, always listening capabilities are needed to act on user-generated sounds.

Description

In this training, we focus on audio data acquisition and it's processing to generate meaningful results. We shall learn the fundamentals of digital audio and audio data acquisition and rendering models. We shall learn basic techniques of audio signal processing and review implementations of some fundamental audio signal processing algorithms. We shall review concepts and try to generate algorithms that make a difference on end user experience.
