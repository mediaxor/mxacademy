Real Time Processing and Multimedia

Embedded software is any computer software which plays an integral role in the electronics with which it is supplied. It can be any code, which interacts with the hardware layer, ranging from the hardware abstraction layer, device drivers, kernel programming to application programming. Telecom, wireless products and consumer electronic products are major verticals in terms of revenue for the embedded software industry. Handheld devices, home multimedia and security systems in consumer electronic segments continue to drive momentum in the embedded multimedia industry.

Automotive

Embedded multimedia systems provide audio and video entertainment in automobiles and rich graphical user interfaces on automobile dashboards. These systems are also known as in-vehicle infotainment (IVI) systems.

Computing

Embedded computing is about executing applications - protocols, signal processing, user interface - while meeting all performance and power requirements on multi-core heterogeneous processor architectures.

Entertainment

Embedded multimedia systems provide audio and video entertainment along with rich graphical user interfaces in consumer products ranging from personal media players to bluetooth audio and mobile devices.

Wearables

Wearable technology can be put on or taken off the body. Embedded technology can be incorporated into the body using wearables, to create an intimate and convenient interface to information and technology.

EMBEDDED SOFTWARE

Growing consumer market is creating a need for localized product features, resulting in increased activity in the embedded software development domain. Multiple embedded processors in SoCs with independent functions are being adopted in electronic devices. While chip designing companies largely work on the hardware abstraction layer and device driver layer of the embedded software, they are also adapting to open source platforms such as Android, which has given a boost to the embedded software industry.

While some companies shorten time-to-market by switching to open source platforms, others prefer to grow their domain specific embedded software expertise in-house.

The embedded software industry is expanding as third party service providers are moving up the value chain to offer more high value activities relating to middleware and driver design services.

Indian vendors are investing and building competencies around new verticals which are set to grow considerably in next few years.

True multimedia is one of the most difficult fields of research as it needs significant knowledge of many disciplines. Over last few years, multimedia research has received tremendous attraction from industry for its widespread reach in user applications. As more and more computing power and functionality is getting pushed into the silicon, there arises a need to standardize the interfaces exposed by these advanced hardware cores.

The Khronos Group is a not for profit industry consortium creating open standards for the authoring and acceleration of parallel computing, graphics, dynamic media, computer vision and sensor processing on a wide variety of platforms and devices. Such initiatives along with the open source software has provided a significant boost to the embedded system industry. Low cost development boards with varying functionalities and computing power are a common platform for modern embedded system education and learning.

Consequently, the growing pervasiveness of multimedia has increased the relevance of its knowledge for computer science and software engineering students.
